Finding a mentor shouldn't
be a struggle

Learning to code can be a challenge

Learning to code as a beginner is not easy. Many beginners struggle along the way in their coding paths. Others don't know if they are headed in the right direction. How do you apply what you're learning to build side projects? You may have peers and colleagues that keep you motivated to keep coding. You may have tutorials and videos online that explain a programming concept in a better way.

Again, you may signup for a bootcamp to help facilitate your coding progress. Finding a coding mentor is aslo a great solution to help you overcome learning obstacles.

Knowledge transfer

Coding mentor passes on their years of experience and knowledge, skills and best practices to the mentee. Coding is not just writing codes but it entails best practices, design patterns and many more.

"Some knowledge comes from years of buiding code not from books and courese."

Improve coding skills

A mentor can spot mistakes in mentees code than themselves. Having a knowledge and experienced mentor is often much more efficient and beneficial for the mentee. This will go a long way to improve their coding skills which leads to time saving and money.

Facilitate growth

Mentors can facilitate and encourage mentee to improve as well as making coding easier for them. Since the mentor has the experience and most likely gone through a similar path as a mentee is undertaken. They are be able to direct them towards helpful careers, books, and programs for growth and development. Again, a mentor can identify areas for improvement, weaker points in a mentee's work which can result in fast growth

In a summary

Coding mentors passes knowledge and experience to mentees. They help to improve coding skills and lastly facilitate growth
